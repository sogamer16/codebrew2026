# Sirius / CosmicWitness — Demo Video Script
### Codebrew 2026 Hackathon

**Suggested runtime: 2–3 minutes**
**Tone: calm, wonder-filled. Don't rush. Let the visuals breathe.**

---

## OPENING (0:00–0:18)

*Screen is black. Fade into the star field slowly.*

**VO:**
"Every moment you've ever lived — a birthday, a first day of school, the day someone you loved was born — that moment sent light out into the universe. And that light is still traveling."

*Stars gently drift into view. The solar system marker glows warmly at center.*

"Sirius lets you find exactly which stars are receiving that light right now."

---

## THE SEARCH (0:18–0:45)

*Type "1969" into the search bar. Hit enter.*

**VO:**
"Type in any year. Any moment in history."

*Stars ripple out from the center. Three float cards pop in automatically.*

"Sirius finds the real stars — from actual NASA star catalogs — whose distance in light-years means they're watching Earth at exactly that moment. Right now, they're seeing the moon landing."

*Hover over a star card. Point out the distance, the spectral type, the constellation.*

"Every star is real. Every distance is calculated. This isn't fiction."

---

## ERA MODE (0:45–1:00)

*Click Era Mode in the dock.*

**VO:**
"Switch on Era Mode and every star is colored by the human era it's currently witnessing — from prehistoric times out to the edge of the modern world."

*Stars shift colors. The era legend glows at the bottom.*

"You can see deep time, painted across the sky."

*Click Era Mode off. Stars return to spectral colors.*

---

## FAMILY CONSTELLATION (1:00–1:40)

*Click the Family button in the dock.*

**VO:**
"This is my favorite part."

*Open Family Constellation modal. Add 2–3 people with birth dates.*

"Enter the birthdays of the people you love. Your parents, your siblings, your kids."

*Click Find Our Stars.*

"Sirius finds the star that witnessed each person being born — the star whose light-travel time puts it watching Earth on the exact day they came into the world."

*The night sky canvas appears with stars plotted. The 3D view shows constellation lines.*

"Then it draws your family as a constellation. Permanent. Written in starlight."

*Click Download Certificate.*

"And you can download a certificate — something real to keep."

---

## FIND IT TONIGHT (1:40–2:00)

*Click Tonight on one of the star cards.*

**VO:**
"Once you find your star, Sirius tells you how to actually see it tonight. Which direction to face. How high up to look."

*The Find It Tonight panel opens showing altitude, azimuth, plain-English directions.*

"Real sky position, calculated for your location, for right now."

---

## CLOSING (2:00–2:20)

*Slowly zoom out in the 3D view. Stars fill the screen. The solar system marker is a tiny warm dot at center.*

**VO:**
"We tend to think of the past as gone. But it's not. Every moment ever lived is still out there, moving through the universe at the speed of light — witnessed by stars we can name, find, and look up at tonight."

*Fade to the Sirius logo.*

"Sirius. Built at Codebrew 2026."

---

## TIPS FOR RECORDING

- Use a screen recorder like OBS (free) or Loom
- Record at 1920×1080, the app looks best full screen
- Go slow on the star ripple animations — let them finish before talking over them
- The Era Mode color shift is subtle; pause on it for 3–4 seconds
- For Family Constellation, pre-fill the dates before hitting record so you're not typing live
- Background music: something ambient and spacey works well — try "Stars" by Roof Music on YouTube (royalty free)
